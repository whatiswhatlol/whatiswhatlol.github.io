My Projects

ASU + GSV Showcase

Working with the team at Arizona State University, Dreamscape Learn, and Hollywood producer Walter Parkes, I developed a Virtual Reality expereince showcased at the 2025 ASU + GSV Summit. My main tasks involved networking, computer graphics, and Development tools. This included networking the completion of a task to the professor, translating a 3500 line HLSL file into Unity Shader Graph, and adding a set of custom toolbar buttons to navigate the experience more efficiently.

How the Planet Became Our World

Working with Arizona State University faculty I develop and design interactive VR class materials, using an internal Unity SDK. These experiences use real world data and ~25 connected students in the same room. Most of my work pertained to VR interactions, and networking.

DSL Cities

DSL Cities is a interactive VR course material used in an upper-division ASU course. I had a heavy hand in the creation of this game, working with faculty to build exactly what they wanted. I did almost all of the programming for this project.

Unstable Star

Working in the Video Game Development Club, I helped with Unity programming for 12 months for this project. Most of my work went into the weapon and ability system. Working with a team of ~100.

FloatVR

For my capstone project, I worked for Brainswitch Labs on FloatVR, a VR Therapy app. I worked on AI 3D model integration that allows a medical professional, or the user alter the experience. I worked in a team of 4 for this project, I mainly handled shader programming and custom server integration.

Hall of Teachers

Hall of Teachers is a currently unreleased exhibit in the Bishops Museum in Honolulu that I helped design and develop. The unique challenge with Hall of Teachers was the expected 10-year lifespan of continuous use, having to make the project as efficient as possible.
